About Me

I'm an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at the University of Southern California, pursuing a combined BS/MS degree with an expected graduation in May 2027.

Currently, I serve as the Electrical Systems Lead for USC Formula Electric Racing, where I oversee electrical systems integration and safety architecture for our competition vehicles. Previously, I co‑founded Neoterium Labs, an engineering maker space where I directed development of innovative projects—including a Metal‑Detecting Rover and a Rubik's‑Cube‑Solving Robot.

My technical interests span PCB design, embedded firmware, power electronics, and AI‑assisted hardware design. I'm passionate about transforming complex engineering challenges into practical, reliable solutions.

Beyond the lab, I enjoy snowboarding, skateboarding, running, hiking, and other adrenaline‑fuelled activities. These adventures keep me balanced and inspire the creative thinking I bring to every project.
