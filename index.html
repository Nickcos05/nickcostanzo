About Me

I'm an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at the University of Southern California, pursuing a combined BS/MS degree with an expected graduation in May 2027.

Currently, I serve as the Electrical Systems Lead for USC Formula Electric Racing, where I oversee electrical systems integration and safety architecture for competition vehicles. Previously, I co-founded Neoterium Labs, an engineering maker space where I directed development of innovative projects including a Metal-Detecting Rover and a Rubiks-Cube Solving Robot.

With experience in PCB design, embedded systems, and electrical engineering, I'm passionate about creating hardware solutions that combine precise engineering with practical applications.

When I'm not working on technical projects, I enjoy Snowboarding, Skateboarding, Running, Hiking, and any other thrillseeking activty!.
